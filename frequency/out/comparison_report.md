## Analysis

### Quality of the comparison

~35% top-1000 agreement is high considering our corpus is a learner-oriented textbook (~3.3K tokens) and Hermitdave's is broad subtitle data (~5K lemmas after aggregation). Diverging items reveal the bias of each:

- **Our deck slants toward textbook didactic items** — verb infinitives (heavily represented because Sakayan teaches them by name), grammatical paradigm cells (`գրեցի, գրել եմ, գրելու եմ` etc., which our pipeline correctly aggregates to `գրել`).

- **Their list slants toward subtitle-translation artifacts** — proper nouns from Shakespeare translations are visible at the top of bucket 3 (`ռոմեո, ջուլիետ, մակբեթ, տիբալտ, մերկուցիո, կավդոր, փեփփ`). These come from Armenian-dubbed films where character names occur at the head of every line. Filter them out when scanning bucket 3 for real gaps.

### Real gaps in our deck (bucket 3, filtered)

After dropping proper nouns and tokenization-quirk fragments (`նչ` from broken `ի՞նչ`, `ինչո` from `ինչու`, `որտե` from `որտեղ`, etc.), the genuinely-actionable gap list is shorter than 640. Hand-curated highlights:

These are core conversational Armenian that Sakayan doesn't isolate as separate vocab cards — many appear in dialogue text but get buried in the top-1000 once the textbook's primary vocab dominates. Adding them would be low-risk, high-value.

### Lemmatization quality observations

Imperfect on both sides; fixable:

- Both lists show `նչ` (rank 21 hermitdave, fixed in ours) from broken tokenization across `ի՞նչ` — Armenian intra-word punctuation `՞ ՛ ՜ ՝` splits words for naive regexes. Our pipeline now strips them before tokenization; Hermitdave's didn't.
- Long-tail lemma fragments like `այդպե` (= `այդպես`), `քանզ` (= `քանզի`), `սիրո` (= `սիրով` or `սեր` gen.) indicate over-stripping in *our* lemmatizer when applied to forms whose stems we don't know. Fixable with a slightly longer suffix list or a known-lemma override.
- Overall accuracy at the head of the list (top-100) is high; quality degrades into the long tail as expected.
