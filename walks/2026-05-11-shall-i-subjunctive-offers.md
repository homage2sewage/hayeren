# Topic walk: "shall I (do X)?" — 1sg subjunctive as offer / permission-question

**Date**: 2026-05-11
**Operator**: Claude Code, manual walk
**Status**: walk executed; topic proposal at bottom; not yet promoted to `topics/`.

## Motivation

Triggered by the question "how plausible is `տոպրակ տա՞մ` in a store?"
(2026-05-11 session). The 1sg-aorist-subjunctive form (`տամ`, `բերեմ`,
`գնամ`, `փակեմ`…) functions as a *speaker-side offer or
permission-question*: literally "(that) I do X," idiomatically "shall
I do X?" / "may I do X?" The English "shall I…?" / Russian
"(мне)…?" / "можно…?" line up almost 1:1.

No `topics/morphology/` or `topics/pragmatics/` file covers this
construction directly. The shape is well-attested across all four
source books; this walk surfaces the passages and proposes a topic.

## Form

- **Lemma**: any verb. The form is the **1sg aorist subjunctive**
  (Sakayan terminology). Class-I (`ե`-conjugation) → `-եմ`
  (`բերեմ, ասեմ, փակեմ, բացեմ`). Class-II (`ա`-conjugation) → `-ամ`
  (`տամ, գնամ, մնամ, կարդամ`).
- **Question marking**: the Armenian `՞` falls on the stressed
  syllable of the focused word (orthographic convention; not a
  semantic marker). E.g. `բերե՞մ`, `տա՞մ`, `փակե՞մ`. Misplacement
  (`տամ՞`) is a typing/orthography slip, not ungrammaticality.
- **Negation**: `չ-` prefix on the verb gives the negated offer
  ("shall I *not* X?" / "should I avoid X?"). Sakayan's paradigm
  spread `(չ)տամ, (չ)տաս, (չ)տա…` (p175) formalises this as part
  of the cell.

## Attestations

### sakayan

- **p105**, bbox y[249.04–260.04]
  > `Պատուհանը փակե՞մ։`
  "Shall I close the window?" — bare offer.

- **p175**, y[527–538]
  > `(չ)տամ, (չ)տաս, (չ)տա, (չ)տանք, (չ)տաք, (չ)տան`
  Aorist-subjunctive paradigm of `տալ`, including the negated
  series. Anchors form-recognition.

- **p176**, bbox y[116.52–127.67]
  > `Ասե՞մ, թե…`
  "Shall I tell (you), or…" — offer chained to an alternative.

- **p189**, bbox y[324.04–335.04]
  > `Սուրճ բերե՞մ։`
  "Shall I bring coffee?" — direct service-encounter analog.
  This is the closest book-attested parallel to the
  `Տոպրակ տա՞մ` cashier case that motivated the walk.

- **p304**, y[613-624]
  > `Քույրս ասաց, որ զանգ տամ իրեն, երբ գործս վերջացնեմ։`
  "My sister said I should call her when I finish my work."
  Embedded subordinate use of the same form — same morphology,
  reportive frame instead of question intonation.

- **p337**, bbox y[578.27–589.27]
  > `Գնա՞մ, թե՞ մնամ։`
  "Shall I go, or stay?" — offer-pair with the alternative
  marked by a second `՞`.

- **p406**, y[169-180]
  > `Բե՛ր, որ քո գտնելու վարձը տամ։`
  "Bring it, so that I (may) give you your finder's fee." —
  purpose-clause use, again same form.

- **p414**, bbox y[393.68–404.68]
  > `Որ ձին քշեմ՝ կհասնե՞մ։`
  "If I drive the horse, will I make it?" — conditional with
  `կ-` prefix; the question lands on the apodosis.

### parnasyan

- **p258**, bbox y[1500–1658] (OCR conf 83.1)
  > `բոլորը գնացել են: ես էլ գնա՞մ։ Все ушли. Можно и мне уйти?`
  "Everyone has left; shall I leave too? — Все ушли. Можно и мне
  уйти?" The Russian gloss locks the reading: `(ес էլ) գնա՞մ` ↔
  `можно и мне уйти?`, i.e. permission-question / "may I…?"

### tioyan

- **p217**, bbox y[149–196] (OCR conf 80.5)
  > `— Ասե՞մ: — Сказать? Կարդա՞մ: Прочесть? Գնա՞մ: Уйти?`
  Explicit pedagogical exposition of the pattern with Russian
  glosses. The Russian renderings (bare-infinitive +
  question-mark, with optional `(т.е. Мне)` "i.e. me") confirm
  the speaker-self-referential reading. This is the most
  citable single passage for the topic.
  *(Note: `Անխե՞մ` in the raw OCR appears to be an
  Ասե՞մ-misread; visual confirmation against the bitmap pending.)*

- **p220**, bbox y[2045–2090] (OCR conf 91.4)
  > `… Դուռը բացե՞մ։ … Ես շատ եմ ուզում, որ մեր այգում …`
  "Shall I open the door?" — same offer pattern, this time
  with a definite direct object (`դուռը`).

### ghamoyan

No direct *1sg* attestation of the offer form surfaced on this
walk. Ghamoyan p67 has a 1sg `կտամ` ("I'll give") in a threat
context (`-Ընենց կտամ, որ…` "I'll hit (you) so that…"), which is
*future indicative*, not subjunctive — different shape, different
illocution. **Gap**: the colloquial Yerevan corpus does not seed
this topic; the literary/standard sources do all the heavy lifting.

## Russian-translation mapping

The four English/Russian senses the same Armenian form covers in
the cited passages:

| Armenian | English | Russian | Speech-act |
|---|---|---|---|
| `Սուրճ բերե՞մ։` | Shall I bring coffee? | Принести (тебе) кофе? | speaker-side offer |
| `ես էլ գնա՞մ։` (parn. p258) | Shall I go too? | Можно и мне уйти? | permission-question |
| `Գնա՞մ, թե՞ մնամ։` | Shall I go or stay? | (Мне) уйти или остаться? | self-deliberation |
| `որ զանգ տամ իրեն` (sak. p304) | (that) I should call her | чтобы я ей позвонила | embedded subjunctive |

All four are *the same morphology* in Armenian; only the framing
(question / declarative / subordinate) changes. This is the
generative point the topic should make explicit: one form, three
illocutions.
