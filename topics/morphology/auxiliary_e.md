# The auxiliary verb `եմ` (copular and auxiliary uses)

`եմ` (1sg present "I am") is the structurally central verb of
Eastern Armenian — it operates as both **copula** (linking subject
to predicate) and **auxiliary** (combining with participles to
form analytic tenses). Most of the language's tense system passes
through this verb. [#1] [#2]

## Two functional roles

> **Sakayan [#1]**: "The auxiliary verb `եմ` (եմ, ես, է, ենք, եք,
> են) has a largely copular function, i.e. it links the subject to
> its complement."

### Role 1: copula

Links subject to a non-verbal predicate (adjective, noun, locative).

Examples [#1]:

- **Անին հիվանդ է։** — "Ani is sick." (subject + adjective + copula)
- **Նրանք հայ են։** — "They are Armenians." (subject + noun + copula)

Used "to indicate a current state, status, or presence" [#1].
*Habitual* state uses the regular continuative `լինում եմ` (see
`topics/morphology/irregular_verbs.md`).

### Role 2: auxiliary in analytic tenses

Combines with a participle (imperfective `-ում`, perfect `-ել`,
future `-ելու`, etc.) to form most of the tense system.

| participle + aux | tense | example |
|------------------|-------|---------|
| գրում + եմ | present | գրում եմ "I write" |
| գրել + եմ | perfect | գրել եմ "I have written" |
| գրելու + եմ | future | գրելու եմ "I will write" |
| գրում + էի | imperfect | գրում էի "I was writing" |
| գրել + էի | pluperfect | գրել էի "I had written" |

The auxiliary carries **person, number, and tense** information; the
participle carries the lexical content (and aspect, in some
analyses). See `topics/morphology/present_tense.md`,
`topics/morphology/participles.md`.

## The full paradigm

| | present | past |
|--|--------|------|
| 1sg | եմ "I am" | էի "I was" |
| 2sg | ես "you are" | էիր |
| 3sg | է "(s)he/it is" | էր |
| 1pl | ենք "we are" | էինք |
| 2pl | եք "you-pl are" | էիք |
| 3pl | են "they are" | էին |

The negative forms (`չեմ չես չի չենք չեք չեն`) prefix `չ-` and
*move before* the participle — see `topics/morphology/negation.md`.

In **Yerevan colloquial speech**, the 3sg `է` is replaced by `ա` —
see `topics/morphology/colloquial_copula_a.md`.

## Three "to be" verbs

Sakayan opens the auxiliary section by noting that **Armenian has
three verbs equivalent to the English verb "to be"** [#1]. They
divide functional labour:

| verb | function | example |
|------|----------|---------|
| `եմ` | copula + auxiliary | Անին հիվանդ է "Ani is sick" |
| `ունեմ` | possession | գիրք ունեմ "I have a book" |
| `կամ` (defective) | existential / location | Բակում կատու կա "there is a cat in the yard" |

Tioyan [#4] introduces `կամ` explicitly as *"бытийный
нестандартный, недостаточный глагол կամ"* — "the existential
nonstandard defective verb կամ." Forms: 1sg `կամ` (rare in modern
speech, more literary), 3sg present `կա`, 3sg past `կար`.

`ունեմ` is the possessive analogue (see
`topics/morphology/irregular_verbs.md` — it has both the short
irregular paradigm and a regular continuative `ունենում եմ`).

The split makes Armenian *finer-grained than English* on the
"to-be" axis — English collapses copular, possessive, and
existential into one verb (`is, has, exists/there is`). Russian
similarly has this three-way split (есть as copula in some uses,
иметь as possessive in others, plus expressed by case constructions).

## Russian-L1 contrastive note

> **Parnasyan [#3]**: "В отличие от русского языка, армянский
> вспомогательный глагол է имеет формы настоящего времени"
>
> "Unlike Russian, the Armenian auxiliary verb է has present-tense
> forms."

Russian's present-tense copula is **null** (`он студент` "he [is] a
student" — no copula). Armenian *requires* the copula in present
tense (`Նա ուսանող է` "He [is] a student"). This is the single
biggest Russian-L1 trap: dropping the copula because Russian has
no overt one.

For full Russian-L1 contrast see `topics/morphology/present_tense.md`
which covers the present-tense use in detail.
